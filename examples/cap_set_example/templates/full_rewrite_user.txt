## STRUCTURE-ORIENTED GUIDANCE

The evaluator prefers cap sets that display clear mathematical structure
(for example: symmetry, regular block/tiling patterns, orbit decompositions,
affine/subspace constructions, or other compact parametric families). When
producing a solution, favor constructions that expose and exploit such
structure rather than merely listing many individual vectors.

Guidelines:

- **Prefer structural descriptions:** produce generators or parametric
  constructions (rules, formulas, or small building blocks) that generate
  the set, instead of embedding long explicit lists of vectors.
- **Encourage symmetry and invariance:** constructions that are invariant
  under coordinate permutations, cyclic shifts, or group actions are valuable.
- **Use compact encodings:** describe the set as orbits, repeated tiles,
  cartesian products, affine translates, or other compact encodings when
  possible.
- **Structure summary:** include a short bullet summary naming the key
  structural features (e.g., "cyclic shifts of base block B", "decomposes
  into 3 orbits under rotation", "product of smaller cap sets").
- **Reproducibility:** if randomness is used, expose `_RANDOM_SEED` and make
  default runs deterministic so the construction can be replayed.

Examples of structural ideas (non-exhaustive and optional): coordinate-wise
patterns, cyclic/group orbits, product constructions, affine-subspace
restrictions, block-tilings, and parameterized templates.

Programs that provide a compact, structural description of the cap set and
an accompanying short explanation of the invariants will be scored more
favorably than programs that only output large enumerations.
